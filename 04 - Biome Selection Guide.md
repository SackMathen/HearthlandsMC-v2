# 04 - Biome Selection Guide
*Last updated: 2026-04-01*

## Biomesoplenty 1.20.1 Biome Curation for Fantasy Theme

Biomesoplenty adds 80+ biomes. For your medieval fantasy server, here's a curated list of which ones to **ENABLE** and which to **DISABLE** to keep your world cohesive.

---

## ✅ RECOMMENDED: Enable These Biomes

These fit your castle/fantasy aesthetic perfectly:

### Forest & Nature
- **Mystic Grove** — Magical forest with mystical feel. Perfect for fantasy lore.
- **Cherry Blossom Grove** — Pretty, peaceful. Good for settlements and roleplay areas.
- **Maple Woods** — Warm, autumn-feeling forest. Cozy atmosphere.
- **Boreal Forest** — Cold, evergreen forest. Good for northern kingdoms/regions.
- **Coniferous Forest** — Dense pine forest. Works well for dark woods/wilderness.
- **Old Growth Woodland** — Ancient, gnarled trees. Very fantasy-feeling.

### Grassland & Meadow
- **Lavender Field** — Pretty, peaceful open area. Great for villages and farming.
- **Clover Patch** — Open meadow. Soft, inviting for settlements.
- **Highland Moor** — Open hillside with heather. Good for castles on elevated terrain.
- **Grassland** — Basic grassy biome. Works as farmland/open land.

### Swamp & Wet
- **Mire** — Spooky swamp with dark trees. Good for mysterious/evil faction territories.
- **Bayou** — Southern swamp. Atmospheric alternative to normal swamps.
- **Bog** — Peaty wetland. Eerie but beautiful.

### Mountain & Rock
- **Highland** — Hilly terrain with elevated plateaus. Excellent for castle placement.
- **Crag** — Rocky, jagged terrain. Dramatic for fortifications.
- **Jade Cliffs** — Unique jade-colored cliffs. Fantasy landmark potential.

### Underground/Cave
- **Glowing Grotto** — Caves with glowing vegetation. Underground fantasy dungeon feel.
- **Crystalline Chasm** — Crystal caves. Very magical and unique.

---

## ❌ DISABLE: These Don't Fit the Theme

These feel too alien, sci-fi, or modern for medieval fantasy:

- **Erupting Inferno** — Volcanic hellscape, too alien
- **Fungal Jungle** — Overly magical/weird, clashes with medieval vibe
- **Lush Savanna** — Tropical, doesn't fit medieval Europe
- **Lush Desert** — Too colorful/vibrant, breaks immersion
- **Dead Forest** / **Old Growth Dead Forest** — Too dark/depressing (keep Mire for that vibe instead)
- **Dryland** — Desert biome, doesn't fit
- **Bamboo Grove** — Asian aesthetic, clashes with medieval European theme

---

## 🟡 OPTIONAL: Enable If You Want More Variety

These are neutral or could work depending on your vision:

- **Mediterranean Forest** — Could work for southern/warm regions
- **Forested Field** — Open forest mix, good for diversity
- **Floodplain** — Riverine biome, could work for river valleys
- **Field** — Open grassland, generic but fine
- **Fir Clearing** — Forest clearing, could work

## Configuration Strategy

### Step 1: Enable/Disable in Config
When you launch the server, Biomesoplenty creates a config file. In that file:
- Set your **recommended biomes to enabled**
- Set **disabled biomes to false**
- Leave **optional biomes** disabled initially; enable them later if you want

### Step 2: Adjust Biome Frequency
For each enabled biome, you can set:
- **Rarity** — how common the biome is (lower = rarer, higher = more common)
- **Size** — how large biome patches are
- **Temperature/Humidity** — where in the world it spawns (hot/cold, wet/dry)

**Suggestion for balance:**
- Make **Mystic Grove** and **Mire** relatively rare (unique, special)
- Make **Lavender Field** and **Highland** more common (good for settlements)
- Make **Boreal Forest** and **Coniferous Forest** appear in northern regions
- Make **Cherry Blossom** and **Maple Woods** appear scattered (special)

### Step 3: Use Terrablender (Optional)
If you add Terrablender mod, you can control **biome regions**:
- North = Boreal Forest, Coniferous Forest, Highland Moor
- Central = Lavender Field, Clover Patch, Maple Woods, Mystic Grove
- South = Highland, Crag, Cherry Blossom
- Swamps = Mire, Bayou, Bog scattered throughout

This creates a more cohesive world where regions feel distinct.
